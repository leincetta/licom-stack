A collaborative project to translate and facilitate the most relevant articles about the design of the user experience in Spanish.

A passion project for giving back

In 2013 I moved from Buenos Aires to Denver. That’s when I realized that all the work I did for the past years was called UX design. Of course, I wanted to know more and learn all about it.

After five years in America, I decided that is time to contribute to the community since all I learned is due to the thousands of authors that help mentor other designers online.

Goal: inclusive literature

Even though there are more Spanish speakers than English, The biggest influencers of this topic write their content in English.

I wanted to reach out to the writers of cornerstone content so they could have their voice amplified in Latin America.

Who is interested in UXÑ?

After interviewing a handful of bilingual designers, I came up with two main personas that represent our users needs.

Victoria

Beginner (Spanish speaking) student.

She lives in Peru and is a first-generation college student. She is open to new ideas, traveling frequently and attending to design meetups at least once a month. Her motivation is to find influencers and resources so she can join the workforce and become the best at her job.

Alex

Advanced (bilingual) student.

He was born in Venezuela and move to America five years ago. An experienced designer, owner of a studio with the need of continually learning how to solve design problems for his portfolio of clients. Alex reads articles from his office and keeps himself updated.

Analyzing the competition

After running a competitive matrices, it turned out that the biggest players in the Spanish UX were contributors that expressed opinions on their own. None of them offered the ability to get to learn from the biggest influencers in the market.

A key partnership

When presenting this project to my teachers in General Assembly, the central question was how I was planning to mitigate copyright risks.

During the research phase of content gathering, most of the articles I was willing to translate came from UX Magazine. Because they are in Denver, we interviewed at their offices so I could explain the project to them. They didn’t only like it, but also expressed their desire of collaborating with UXÑ, by giving total permission for translating their content. The commitment was:

1. To credit the authors
2. To post only after the original post is online for two months.
3. To remove content that the author thinks it's copyright infringement.

UX Magazine. One of the top publishers in the industry of UX. Their generosity allowed me to move forward with my project.
