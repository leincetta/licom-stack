Introducing all-new features

Through the online portal, customers can submit and approve press releases for distribution, track order status and history, and access online media distribution results. Companies can disseminate corporate press releases more conveniently to reach over 300,000 media outlets in more than 170 countries via PR Newswire’s global distribution network.

  • Manage

    Manage your assets

    Customers can upload images to a media studio and conveniently select and re-use media assets to be included in the press release distribution.

    Imagine having to submit an image with your logo via email every time you need to send a press release.

  • Send

    Send a release

    Online submission of press releases, images and other supporting documents in Excel and PDF formats.

    Before this product, users had to submit their text and media assets to PR Newswire via email. Then they will enter this tedious loop of content corrections in never-ending threads.

  • Track

    Track your order

    Customers can in real-time view, manage and follow up on the status of their release orders. This feature offers a hassle-free and quicker way than using phones and emails to distribute press releases.

    This feature also improves transparency and traceability of order processing.

  • Analyze

    Analyze the outcomes

    Through the portal, customers can access post-release distribution reports that contain a rich set of statistics and analysis of online media coverage over time, location and types, search engine visibility and release views.

    The online platform also allows exporting and downloading of the reports into Word, Excel and PDF formats.

The glow

With the successful launch of this product, I got promoted into Senior Designer. Later I'd become the company's UX Team Manager.

Learned to design within constraints

Blocked scripts, a subset of design components, create a site in Traditional Chinese, 12-hour difference between business owners and the rest of the team...

Fostered team collaboration

To create shared understanding being so far away from each other, with the Product team we relied on visual thinking tools and a lot of communication.

The grow

Being my first corporate job, I had to learn how to navigate through bureaucracy and polish my soft skills in a new language and culture.

Test with real users

Because this was technically a portal to be released in the USA, all the participants were American. However, the end-customers ended up being Chinese people.

Mobile needs aren't web needs

The website was responsive, yes. But it was unlikely that anyone would submit a press release through a phone. Users only wanted to check status and performance.
