Transamerica aspires to enable customers to have a single point of access for all their data and to get a complete picture of where they stand.

On September 2018, I accepted the challenge of becoming the Lead Designer of two different product teams responsible for investigating, creating, and improving the usability of the post-login individual experience.

The goal of this project is to create one single web portal where users can self-service their Retirement, Annuities, and Life Insurance products.

Team 1: Life and Annuities

Transamerica closed a deal with TCS (Tata Consulting Services) to have them storing all of our financial information and providing service APIs. We are building the front end and connecting it to their database.

New front end

The challenge is to create a website that combines the old Annuities website with the legacy Life Insurance web app. In addition to that, the user experience must be similar to the one from PX.

Database migration

The backend team needs to feed from BaNCS, the database from TCS. Business analysts identify the workarounds of our current legacy products to migrate all that information into our new system.

  1. 1

    Check my policies

    Customers come to these websites to check their total account value and their rate of return. These values constantly vary depending on the market fluctuations, and for that reason is that users want to keep an eye on their finances.

  2. 2

    Print/email policies

    The age range for a person with an annuity is 65+ years old. Out of use and for peace of mind, people like to print their policy details on paper. An alternative to this is email.

  3. 3

    Allocate funds

    Although this is an operation that financial advisors do, some of our customers want to have the ability of performing these tasks by themselves.

Thank goodness for design systems

Transamerica's Cardinal Pod ensures that we are on brand. That we don't create component from scratch. And a seamless user experience across Pods.

Team 2: Retirement

As part of the Workplace Experience, our legacy websites that are the product of multiple company mergers, need a 2019 update.

Help the beginners

The array of customers is broad. Financial websites can be intimidating and full of jargon. Transamerica needs to enable and engage those customers who are starting their financial life journey by providing clear directions.

Advise the retirees

The most seasoned customers use Transamerica as a tool. They usually work with a financial advisor, have financially planned their lives, but still don’t know how our products can benefit them.

  1. 1

    Check my account

    Enrollment, signup. Verification of values, rate of return, and outlook. Customers want to have the ability of printing their statements and checking how their accounts are performing.

  2. 2

    Allocate money

    Financial transactions such as rollovers, withdrawals, and loans.

  3. 3

    Make adjustments

    Our users want to be able to edit their beneficiaries and edit their personal information.
